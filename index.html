Walla Walla, Washington

Welcome to Walla Walla

Walla Walla is a small, walkable wine city tucked into the southeastern corner of Washington — about 34,000 people, a college town with genuine small-town energy, and a food and wine scene that punches well above its weight. What makes it special isn't just the 130+ wineries; it's the fact that the winemakers are often pouring in their own tasting rooms, the restaurants are locally owned and chef-driven, and the whole thing feels like a community rather than a wine resort. There's no pretense here — just really good wine, great food, and people who are happy you came.

May is a sweet spot: warm enough for vineyard patios, green hills before the summer heat sets in, and a calendar full of spring energy without the summer crowds.

Best Time to VisitMay–June for mild temps, green vineyards, and fewer crowds. October for harvest energy and fall colors. Avoid August — it gets hot.

Recommended Stay3–4 nights gives you time to explore downtown, hit the Southside and Eastside wineries, and actually relax.

From BoiseAbout 252 miles, 4 hours via I-84 West and US-12 North. Note: Walla Walla is on Pacific Time — 1 hour behind Boise.

The Valley at a Glance

The name "Walla Walla" comes from a Sahaptin term most often translated as "many waters" — a nod to the rivers and creeks that crisscross the valley. It's an apt name. The region is lush and green in spring, defined by the Blue Mountains rising to the east. The wine region officially spans two states, with the wineries mostly in Washington but a meaningful share of the vineyard acreage across the Oregon border. The AVA was first designated in 1984 — one of the earliest in Washington — and the valley has been building its reputation ever since.

Neighborhoods & Districts

Downtown / Main Street

The heart of it all. The Finch Hotel sits right on Main Street — steps from 40+ walkable tasting rooms, the best restaurants, boutique shops, and coffee. Flat sidewalks, easy stroller access, and a genuine small-city energy.

Southside

Where the scenery opens up. Rolling hills, estate vineyards, and Blue Mountain views in every direction. Home to Caprio Cellars, Amavi, and Valdemar. About 10–15 minutes from downtown by car.

Eastside / Mill Creek

A quieter, more scenic drive into the foothills at the base of the Blue Mountains. Home to Echolands, Walla Walla Vintners, and Abeja. About 15 minutes from downtown — Echolands in particular has the best views in the region.

Airport District

A former WWII airfield converted into a casual cluster of tasting rooms — Dunham Cellars is the anchor. Artsy, low-key vibe with a lot of character. About 10 minutes northeast of downtown.

Westside

Some of the valley's oldest wineries on the approach road into town — Woodward Canyon and L'Ecole are here. Also home to Foundry Vineyards, the art-forward tasting room that's a completely different experience from a traditional stop. About 10 minutes from downtown.

Winery Guide

These picks were chosen for a group traveling with babies — which means outdoor space, relaxed hospitality, seated experiences, and no need to stand at a bar while wrangling a stroller. All confirmed open for the May 14–17 window. Reservations strongly recommended for all, especially for a group of six.

🏆 The Must-Do Stops
Echolands Winery
Eastside / Mill Creek · Open Wed–Mon 11am–5pm · $20/flight, waived with 2-bottle purchase

The showstopper. Opened April 2024 on a 341-acre estate in the Mill Creek foothills — the highest-elevation winery in Walla Walla — with a chic, modern tasting room framed by floor-to-ceiling windows and panoramic Blue Mountain views. The kind of place that stops conversation mid-sip. Kids explicitly welcome (they even have a zero-proof flight for non-drinkers). Book inside seating specifically — overflow guests have been seated outside with a reduced experience.

Vibe: Architecture-forward, modern, spectacular views. The biggest "wow" in the valley right now.

Drive from hotel: ~15 minutes

Book via: Tock or call (509) 676-2109. Request indoor seating for 6 adults + babies when booking.

Caprio Cellars
Southside · Open Wed–Sun 11am–5pm · No tasting fee — complimentary with paired small plates

One of the most distinctive tasting experiences in Walla Walla. Greeted with sparkling wine at the gate, then seated on a patio or inside with 360° valley views while a chef-prepared small plate arrives with each pour. Bordeaux-style blends, exceptional hospitality, and the energy of a dinner party rather than a tasting room. No tasting fee — the food is included. Four seatings daily: 11am, 12:30pm, 2pm, 3:30pm. By appointment only. Note: Caprio describes itself as a family-owned winery that welcomes families — call ahead to confirm they can accommodate the group's babies on the day of your visit.

Vibe: Elevated, seated, food-forward. Everyone stays put, which is ideal with babies.

Drive from hotel: ~12 minutes

Book via: Tock or call (509) 412-3054.

🍷 Great Supporting Stops
Amavi Cellars
Southside · Open daily, walk-ins welcome as space allows · Reservations recommended for groups

Explicitly welcomes families, large groups, and dogs. The patio overlooks the Pepper Bridge Estate Vineyard with Blue Mountain views — spacious and easy to navigate with strollers. Known for estate Sémillon, Syrah, and a seasonal Cab Franc Rosé. Pairs well with Caprio for a full Southside Saturday.

Vibe: Relaxed, beautiful, genuinely welcoming. Consistently top-rated.

Drive from hotel: ~12 minutes

Book via: amavicellars.com or walk in

Foundry Vineyards
Westside · Open Thu–Sat 11am–5pm, Sun noon–4pm · $25/flight, waived with 2-bottle purchase · Walk-ins welcome

Part winery, part art gallery, part design shop — set in a converted WWII-era foundry building with a contemporary sculpture garden and rotating quarterly exhibitions. Families explicitly welcome. Organic and natural wines including pet-nats under the Pét Project label. A completely different energy from a traditional winery stop. A short drive from The Finch Hotel.

Vibe: Art-forward, creative, cool. The spot for anyone as interested in design as in what's in the glass.

Drive from hotel: ~8 minutes

Book via: Walk-ins welcome; foundryvineyards.com for info

🚶 Downtown Tasting Rooms (Walking Distance)
L'École Nº 41 — Heritage Wine Bar
Downtown · Marcus Whitman Hotel · Walk-ins welcome · Open daily · ~10 min walk from The Finch Hotel

One of Walla Walla's founding wineries (est. 1983) with a downtown Heritage Wine Bar location inside the Marcus Whitman Hotel — no driving required. Flights and glass pours from a winery widely considered the benchmark for Walla Walla Bordeaux-style reds and arguably America's best Semillon. Easy, relaxed, historic. A great Friday afternoon first stop right off the hotel's doorstep.

Vibe: Approachable, historic. Walk in, sit down, taste some of the valley's most reliable wines.

The Walls Vineyards
5 min from Downtown · Open daily · Walk-ins embraced · Lunch menu available all day · ~5 min drive from The Finch Hotel

Just outside the walkable core, but worth the short drive or ride. Modern tasting room with a diverse portfolio and a lunch menu served all day — making it the smartest combined lunch-and-taste stop on the trip. Reservations recommended but genuinely flexible. Good option for a relaxed midday break when nap schedules are unpredictable.

Vibe: Relaxed, practical, well-regarded. Eat and taste in one stop.

Seven Hills Winery
Downtown · Whitehouse-Crawford Building · Walk-ins welcome · ~5 min walk from The Finch Hotel

One of the founding wineries of the Walla Walla region, located in the beautifully restored 1904 Whitehouse-Crawford building — a National Register of Historic Places landmark. Strong Merlot and Cabernet Sauvignon-based wines that authentically reflect the valley's terroir. Historic setting, walkable, and a great complement to the L'Ecole stop for a downtown tasting afternoon.

Vibe: Historic, architectural, easy going. A classic Walla Walla stop.

⚠️ EVEREN Wines — Brand new Southside winery (opened Aug 2025) with arguably the best architecture and design in the valley. However, they do not accommodate minors on the property and cap groups at 4 people. Not an option for this trip — but a strong recommendation for a future couples trip without kids.

Where to Eat

Walla Walla's restaurant scene is impressive for a town this size — locally owned, chef-driven, and connected to the local farm community. The top dinner spots book out on weekends, so have reservations locked before leaving Boise. With babies, early seatings (5–5:30pm) are the move.

☕ Breakfast & Coffee
Carte Coffee
Specialty Coffee · Downtown · $

The most serious coffee in Walla Walla. A multi-roaster operation that rotates through a new single-origin espresso every two weeks, with baristas who genuinely know what they're doing. Sleek, modern space at 8 N Colville St — easy walk from The Finch Hotel. Raved about by coffee nerds and casual drinkers alike.

Open: Daily 7am–4/5pm

Order: Whatever single-origin is on espresso, London Fog, cortado

Coffee Perk
Coffee / Bakery · Downtown · $

A downtown Walla Walla institution since 1995 — one of the oldest espresso cafes in the city. Located right in the heart of downtown overlooking the First Avenue Plaza. Locally roasted beans, a pastry case stocked with baked goods daily, and a cozy atmosphere. Great if you want something more relaxed and neighborhood-y than Carte.

Open: Daily, morning hours

Order: Espresso drink, pastry of the day

Maple Counter Cafe
American Breakfast · Downtown · $$

The consensus best breakfast in Walla Walla. Big portions, housemade batters, and pancakes people talk about long after they leave. Join the Google waitlist before leaving the hotel — Saturday mornings will have a line.

Open: Daily 7am–3pm (breakfast all day; lunch weekdays only; breakfast only on weekends)

Order: Buttermilk pancakes, eggs Benedict, biscuits and gravy

Reservations: No — Google waitlist only

🍽 Lunch & Dinner

Casual

The Walls Vineyards — Lunch on-site
American / Wine Country · 5 min from Downtown · $–$$

The smartest lunch play for a group with babies: eat and taste at the same stop. Lunch menu served all day, every day in the tasting room — no extra reservation needed, no second car trip, and the babies can settle in while the adults taste.

Open: Daily, lunch all day

Reservations: Recommended for group; walk-ins embraced

Yamas Greek Eatery
Greek · Downtown · $$

Authentic Greek food made from family recipes right in the heart of downtown — one of the most approachable and consistently praised spots in Walla Walla. Great for lunch or an early dinner, especially with babies in tow: casual, quick service, generous portions, and an outdoor terrace. A nice break from wine country fare when the group wants something lighter and fresh.

Open: Daily 11am–8pm

Order: Lamb gyro, chicken souvlaki plate, spanakopita, housemade hummus and tzatziki, fried octopus

Reservations: Available via Resy; walk-ins welcome

Mid-Range

Hattaway's on Alder
Southern / Pacific Northwest · Downtown · $$

Southern cooking traditions — Gullah, low country, Daufuskie — remade with Pacific Northwest ingredients. House-cured meats, local seafood, excellent cocktails, and one of the most attentive service teams in town. Reserve early for a group of 6.

Open: Daily from 4pm (earlier close Mon/Tue/Wed/Sun, 9pm Thu–Sat)

Order: Wine barrel-smoked salmon, pulled pork shoulder, crab pasta, charcuterie

Reservations: Required — Resy or hattawaysonalder.com

Passatempo Taverna
Italian · Downtown · $$

House-made pasta, wood-fired pizza, and one of the best bar programs in town. The outdoor patio is serene and bamboo-lined — a great atmosphere for an early group dinner. Reserve well in advance for 6 — they book via Resy or by phone.

Open: Thursday–Monday, 4pm–9:30pm (closed Tue/Wed)

Order: Carbonara, rigatoni bolognese, margherita pizza, focaccia, housemade gelato

Reservations: Required — Resy or (509) 876-8822

Saffron Mediterranean Kitchen
Mediterranean · Downtown · $$$

The most celebrated restaurant in Walla Walla, open since 2007 and still the most reliably excellent dinner in town. Turkish flatbreads, housemade pastas, fresh seafood, and an outstanding valley wine list. Saturday is the window for this group — book early.

Open: Tuesday–Saturday only (closed Sun/Mon). Friday opens at 2pm, Tue–Thu 4:30pm.

Order: Gözleme flatbread, braised beef cheek, pasta, anything the server recommends

Reservations: Required — Tock or (509) 525-2112

Sweet Stops

Pine Cone Creamery
Ice Cream · Downtown · $

Small-batch, handcrafted ice cream from the family behind Maple Counter. 18 rotating flavors including vegan options. A short walk from The Finch Hotel — the natural end to any afternoon or evening out.

Open: Daily noon–9pm (until 10pm Fri/Sat)

Order: Honey lavender, whatever the seasonal special is

Things To Do

Beyond the wineries, Walla Walla has just enough to fill the non-tasting hours — especially for a group with babies who need flat, stroller-friendly ground and regular nap windows.

🌿 Nature & Outdoor
Pioneer Park

Walla Walla's oldest park (est. 1902) — 58 acres with 1.4 miles of paved, ADA-accessible paths, a duck pond, playground, rose garden, and a free aviary with peacocks and exotic birds. Completely flat, fully stroller-friendly, and one of the genuinely great small-city parks in the Pacific Northwest. Free, open 5am–11pm daily. About a 10-minute drive from The Finch Hotel, or a pleasant 20-minute stroller walk. Best for a morning stroll on Saturday or Sunday before heading out for the day.

Whitman College Campus Walk

A 10-minute walk from The Finch Hotel through a beautiful, shaded college campus. The self-guided sculpture walk features 21 works, many created by Whitman alumni. Flat campus paths, totally stroller-accessible, and easy to cut short. Great Sunday morning option before checkout. Free, always open.

🛍 Shopping
Downtown Farmers Market
4th & Main · Saturdays 9am–1pm (May through October) · ~12 min walk from The Finch Hotel

Running every Saturday through the season, this market brings local produce, artisan goods, and food vendors to the heart of downtown. A great Saturday morning start before heading to wineries — grab snacks, local honey, or flowers. Stroller-friendly and very manageable in size.

Liz + Kay Clothing Co.
213 E Alder St · Downtown · Open daily (Mon/Sun until 3pm, Tue–Sat until 6pm)

A locally owned women's boutique founded by cousins Brooklynn and Lindsee. Curated, affordable, accessible style with new arrivals weekly and a selection of jewelry from local Walla Walla makers. A short walk from The Finch Hotel.

Sweetwater Paper & Home
101 E Alder St · Downtown · Open Mon–Sat 10am–5pm (closed Sunday)

A highly-loved gift shop that's been a downtown staple for years. Cards, stationery, home goods, handcrafted jewelry, candles, wine accessories, and Walla Walla-themed souvenirs. The kind of store where you go in for one thing and leave with five. Great for picking up something for the people back home or treating yourself mid-trip.

Walla Walla Clothing Company
Downtown · Open daily

A well-established downtown boutique carrying quality women's and men's clothing — brands like Eileen Fisher, AG Jeans, Mother Denim, Vince, and Free People. More investment-piece shopping than impulse buying, with a stylish edit and experienced staff. A good stop if you want something you'll actually wear long after the trip.

Inland Octopus
Downtown

Walla Walla's beloved independent toy store, carrying an eclectic mix of toys, gifts, and games for all ages. Great for picking up something small for the babies or for kids back home. A fun browse even if you don't buy anything.

Practical Info

Getting Here & Around
  • Boise → Walla Walla: ~252 miles, about 4 hours via I-84 West and US-12 North
  • Walla Walla is on Pacific Time — 1 hour behind Boise. Factor into your Sunday drive-home window
  • A car is required for Southside and Eastside wineries. Downtown is fully walkable from The Finch Hotel
  • No reliable rideshare outside downtown — own vehicles are the way to go
  • Cell signal can drop outside downtown — download Maps and winery addresses before leaving the hotel
The Finch Hotel — Baby Flag
  • ⚠️ At least one booking platform notes cribs/infant beds are NOT available — confirm directly before arrival
  • Call (509) 956-4994 or text the front desk (they offer a text check-in system) before the trip
  • If they can't provide cribs, plan to bring pack-n-plays or arrange a local rental
Winery Booking Tips
  • Echolands, Caprio: book via Tock — and specifically request indoor seating for Echolands
  • Hattaway's, Passatempo: book via Resy before leaving Boise — these fill up fast on weekends
  • Saffron: closed Sunday and Monday — Saturday is the only window if you want to go
  • Maple Counter: no reservations — join the Google waitlist from the hotel before walking over
  • Call Caprio ahead of time to confirm they can accommodate babies the day of your visit
  • Tasting fees typically $20–35/person — most waived with a 2-bottle purchase. Caprio has no fee.
May Weather
  • Average highs 66–70°F, lows around 45°F — mornings and evenings are cool, bring layers for the babies
  • About 27% daily chance of rain — most winery patios have covered areas, pack a light jacket
Helpful Apps
  • Tock — reservations for Echolands, Caprio, Saffron, and others
  • Resy — Hattaway's and Passatempo
  • Walla Walla Valley Wine app — filterable winery guide by district, varietal, and hours
